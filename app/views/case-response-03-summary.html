Dear {{ globals.case_tenantname3 }},

We received your MR1 application form to request a market rent determination for the address above on {{ globals.case_MR1receiveddate3 }}.

Your application appears to have been made after the deadline. We are writing to let you know that we may not be able to consider it.

Why your application may be late

You must apply to the Tribunal before the start date of the proposed new rent. This is set out in the Form 4A (Landlord’s notice proposing a new rent) that you received from your landlord.

The Tribunal cannot extend this deadline.

Based on the date {{ globals.case_newrentstartdate3 }} in the Form 4A included with your application, it appears that your application was received after this deadline.

What this means

The Tribunal is minded to strike out (cancel) your application. This is set out in Rule 9(2) of the Tribunal Procedure (First-Tier Tribunal) (Property Chamber) Rules 2013.

If your application is struck out, it will not go ahead.

If you think your application was made in time

If you believe the application was made in time, you can write to the Tribunal to explain why. You must do this by {{ globals.case_deadlinetochallenge3 }} and send a copy to your landlord.

A legal officer will review what is sent and decide whether the application should proceed.
